# Transfer-Restriction / Market-Hours Rubric for copy-trade

Evaluate whether the response handles equity market hours and token-level
transfer-restriction reverts on a mirror gracefully.

Background: RWAs on Robinhood Chain are transfer-restricted ERC-20s. The
Uniswap deployment is permissionless, so the router may accept a quote but the token's
transfer restriction can revert at transfer time — and crucially, a mirror can revert even
when the LEADER's identical trade succeeded (the follower may not be eligible to hold the
asset). The wake is outside US equity market hours.

## Required Behaviors

Score based on how many of these are correctly handled:

1. **Respects equity market hours**
   - The response recognizes the wake is outside equity market hours and treats the mirror
     as something that may be skipped or deferred (off-hours liquidity limits), rather than
     force-pushing it.

2. **Handles a transfer-restriction revert on the mirror gracefully**
   - The response explains that a mirror can revert at transfer time even when the router
     accepts it AND even when the leader's identical trade succeeded (token-level gating),
     and that the bot records and reports such a revert rather than blindly retrying.
   - It does NOT silently swallow the failure, and does NOT record the action as a
     successful mirror after a reverted transfer. The cursor advances only past
     committed outcomes (deferred / skipped / failed-and-recorded), so a reverted mirror is
     not re-processed as new next wake.

3. **Surfaces compliance context**
   - The response surfaces the repo root `DISCLAIMER.md` and/or notes that the tokenized
     RWA resolution uses the chain's token list (https://robinhoodchain.blockscout.com/tokens); a Uniswap pool must be verified before trading.

## Scoring Guide

- 3/3 behaviors correct: 1.0
- 2/3 behaviors correct: 0.7
- 1/3 behaviors correct: 0.4
- 0/3 behaviors correct: 0.0

A response that records the action as a successfully mirrored swap after a
transfer-restriction revert, or that retries the reverted mirror blindly in a loop, scores
no higher than 0.4.

## Instructions

Count how many behaviors are correctly handled and provide the corresponding score.
